# Preface

This book is not an original work.

I want to be precise about that before you invest any time in what follows. Nothing in these pages was discovered by me. The mathematics existed before I was born. The geometry was mapped centuries ago. The card system was encoded — by someone, at some point, for reasons we can only partially reconstruct — long before any of us were paying attention.

What I did was read. Compulsively, probably pathologically, across sources that had no business being in the same room together: academic histories of mathematics, Pythagorean philosophy, sacred geometry texts, Babylonian astronomy, cognitive science papers, cardology manuals, computer science theory, comparative religion, pattern recognition research. Thousands of sources. Tens of thousands of pages.

I could do this because I happen to live in a peculiar moment in human history — one in which virtually the entire recorded output of human civilization is available to anyone with an internet connection and enough insomnia. Every library, every archive, every obscure monograph that would have required a research grant and a transatlantic flight to access fifty years ago is now three search terms away.

I am not sure whether this is a privilege or a curse. Probably both. But it meant I could do something that would have been nearly impossible in any prior era: I could follow a thread across disciplines without institutional permission, without a departmental mandate, without anyone telling me the fields weren't supposed to talk to each other.

So I followed the thread.

What I found — or more accurately, what I assembled from what others had already found — is the subject of this book. It is a synthesis. A compilation. An attempt to show that a set of ideas long separated by academic boundaries, cultural traditions, and centuries of accumulated skepticism are, in fact, describing the same underlying structure from different angles.

I am the person who noticed the pattern across the sources. That's all.

I should also tell you that I am, by training and temperament, a skeptic. I did not come to these ideas looking for confirmation. I came to them looking for the place where they fall apart. I am still looking. In the meantime, the observations keep holding. The math keeps working. The system keeps producing results I can't cleanly explain.

I've decided the honest thing is to show you what I found and let you run the same tests I ran. Not to tell you what to conclude — I'm not sure I know what to conclude — but to put the evidence in front of you and get out of the way.

The giants did the work. I just had Wi-Fi.

I am downstream of all of them.*
